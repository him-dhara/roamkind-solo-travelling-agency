## The Tldr

Built from zero - brand identity, voice, color system, section-by-section copy, image direction, and a full Framer website. One person, start to finish. No handoff.

## The Problem With Most Travel Brands

Waaait... have you noticed how every solo travel brand looks exactly the same??

Either it's a booking engine dressed up with stock photos of couples on beaches.
Or it's a backpacker-budget site that makes solo travel look like a compromise.

Neither makes a first-time solo traveler feel confident enough to actually go.

Neither speaks to the person who travels alone by choice - not by accident.

And NEITHER builds the kind of trust that turns a curious visitor into a booked traveler.

Roamkind was built to fix that. A brand with an actual personality. A voice that said "we've been where you want to go and we'll make sure you get there right."

Bold but not reckless.
Community-first but not soft.
Adventurous but not irresponsible.

## The Brand System

One dark forest green doing ALL the heavy lifting. 🙃

| Role | Token | Hex | Opacity |
|------|-------|-----|---------|
| Background | Background | `#FAF9F6` | 100% |
| Primary | Ascent + Text | `#085041` | 100% |
| Body | Body Text | `#085041` | 80% |
| Border | Border | `#084F40` | 10% |
| Special | Special | `#E1F5EE` | 100% |

Forest green at full opacity for headings and primary UI. The same green at 80% for body text - just enough softness to create hierarchy without introducing a new color. Border at 10% opacity keeps dividers invisible but felt. Special (mist green) for accents and light surface moments.

Disciplined. Nothing wasted.

**Typography:**

| Role | Font | Size | Weight |
|------|------|------|--------|
| H1 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 61px / 1.1 | Regular |
| H2 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 49px / 1.2 | Regular |
| H3 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 39px / 1.3 | Regular |
| H4 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 31px / 1.4 | Regular |
| H5 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 25px / 1.3 | Regular |
| H6 | `Hedvig Letters Serif` | 20px / 1.4 | Regular |
| Body | `General Sans` | 16px / 1.2 | Medium |
| Button | `General Sans` | 16px / 1.2 | Medium |
| Small | `General Sans` | 13px / 1.4 | Medium |

Hedvig Letters Serif for all display - editorial, warm, confident. Feels like a travel journal, not a SaaS dashboard. General Sans for body and UI - clean, geometric, gets out of the way so the copy can land. The contrast between the two is where the brand personality lives.

---

### Hero Section

> *"Go where you've never thought to go"*

Full-bleed hero with a bold Hedvig serif headline sitting over a split image layout - solo traveler portrait left, misty forest and campfire shots right. Dark, cinematic, immediately communicates the world Roamkind lives in.

Two CTAs below the headline - solid primary and ghost secondary. Social proof line underneath with star rating and traveler count. Everything above the fold is doing positioning work before the user has scrolled a single pixel.

---

### About Section

> *"Roamkind is a solo travel agency built for people who want the world on their own terms - raw, real, and without compromise."*

Large editorial headline left col, supporting body copy right col. Below - a photo grid of six images spanning landscape, aerial, portrait, detail, architecture, and night shots. No two compositions the same.

Five trust stats sit underneath the grid - solo travelers, destinations, rating, repeat booking rate, and the brand promise stat. Behavioral numbers over generic ones, every time.

---

### Features Section

> *"Everything a solo traveler actually needs."*

Four numbered feature cards in a horizontal layout. Each card carries a number, two-line title, and body copy matched in length across all four. Visual rhythm here was intentional - makes the section feel considered, not assembled.

- **Zero tourist itineraries** - every route built from local knowledge, not TripAdvisor
- **Small groups, real connections** - max 8 people per trip
- **Every detail, completely sorted** - stays, transfers, guides, permits - all handled
- **Designed around the solo traveler** - not couples, not families, not groups of 40

---

### Destination Section

> *"Pick your next disappearance"*

Full-width stacked destination cards - one per destination, each filling the viewport width. Morocco, Vietnam, Georgia, Peru, Japan, Colombia. Every card carries a cinematic landscape photo, destination name in large Hedvig serif, one-line description, and a four-column info bar at the bottom: base price, duration, ideal for, transport.

The bottom info bar is doing the filtering work without a filter UI. Users scan, compare, and self-select - no dropdown needed.

---

### Steps Section

> *"Your solo adventure starts here in four simple steps"*

Left sticky title col, right scrollable card layout. Four steps: Tell us where you want to go - We build the trip from scratch - Meet your fellow roamers - Show up and wander.

Each step card carries a photo, number, title, and body. The copy in every step is objection handling disguised as process explanation. Each step removes a barrier, not just describes a process.

---

### Testimonials Section

> *"Don't just take our word for it"*

Three full quotes top row, two shorter quotes bottom row. Every testimonial carries the traveler's name and which destinations they've done. Specific trip history under names builds more credibility than a star rating - it proves these are real people who actually went.

No stock photos. Avatar initials only. Keeps it human without faking it.

---

### Footer

Four columns: contact info left, Discover links, Trips links, Social links right. Giant "roamkind." wordmark at the very bottom in large Hedvig serif - cream type on near-black background. Closes the page with weight and brand confidence.

## The Copy

Yeppp - every single word on this site was written from scratch. 🙃

Not placeholder text replaced with real words.

Actually written. With a brand voice. With intention.

With the specific person who lands on this page in mind - the solo traveler who's done the tourist trail, tired of package holidays dressed up as experiences, and ready for something real.

The voice rule for Roamkind was simple: talk like a friend who's been everywhere. Not a platform. Not an agency. A person who gets it, has the contacts, and will make sure you don't waste a single day.

Bold. Human. Direct. Zero corporate travel-speak.

---

## The Images

Every image direction was built around one rule - dark forest greens and cool mist as the world, terra cotta on the subject as the human moment.

Shot type variety was non-negotiable across the site - aerial, portrait, detail, architecture, interior, and night - so no two feel compositionally the same.

All prompts were written for Midjourney. Landmark-anchored for destinations (Chefchaouen for Morocco, Gergeti Trinity for Georgia, Machu Picchu for Peru) - shot in Roamkind's cool, misty, cinematic aesthetic rather than the warm golden-hour look every other travel brand defaults to.

## What This Proves

Brand identity, Framer development, and copywriting can all live in the same brain.

Roamkind isn't a website with nice visuals and placeholder copy swapped out. It's a brand with a voice, a point of view, and a genuine reason to exist in a crowded market. Every decision - color, type, copy, image direction, section order - was made with one specific person in mind.

And honestly?? That's the only kind of website worth building. 🙃
